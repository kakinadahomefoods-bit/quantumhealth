Stem Cell Therapy

Understanding the science, the regulation, and our clinical position.

Stem cell therapy is one of the most actively researched areas in regenerative medicine. However, the gap between laboratory research and validated clinical application remains wide. At Swiss Regenerative Klinik, we believe patients deserve a clear understanding of what stem cell therapy can and cannot do today.

What Are Stem Cells?

Stem cells are undifferentiated cells capable of self-renewal and differentiation into specialised cell types. They are found in various tissues throughout the body and play a role in natural repair processes. Research is ongoing into whether stem cells can be harnessed therapeutically for conditions where tissue damage or degeneration is a factor.

Current Regulatory Status

In Switzerland, stem cell therapies are regulated by Swissmedic and the Federal Office of Public Health (FOPH). Only a small number of indications have received approval for clinical use — primarily haematopoietic stem cell transplantation for certain blood disorders. Most other stem cell applications remain in the research phase and are not approved for routine clinical practice.

Swiss regulations require that any stem cell treatment offered outside approved indications be conducted within the framework of a clinical trial, with appropriate ethical approval and patient safeguards.

Our Position

Swiss Regenerative Klinik does not offer stem cell therapies that fall outside the scope of Swiss regulatory approval or that lack a sufficient evidence base. We monitor developments in stem cell research closely and will incorporate validated protocols as they become available through appropriate clinical channels.

We caution patients against travelling abroad for unregulated stem cell treatments that make claims beyond what the current evidence supports. If you are considering stem cell therapy, we recommend discussing the risks, costs, and evidence with a qualified medical professional.
