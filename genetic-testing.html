Genetic Testing & DNA Screening

Next-generation sequencing and targeted DNA analysis to inform personalised treatment planning.

Genetic testing deciphers the information encoded in your DNA. At Swiss Regenerative Klinik, we use next-generation sequencing (NGS) technology to screen for actionable genetic markers that may influence treatment decisions, medication compatibility, and preventive health strategies.

How Genetic Testing Works

Your DNA carries two types of genetic variation relevant to health management:

  • Inherited variants — passed from parents, present from birth, associated with hereditary conditions and familial disease risk
  • Somatic variants — acquired over time due to environmental factors, toxins, or aging

Identifying these variants allows your clinician to tailor screening intervals, avoid contraindicated medications, and design a treatment protocol aligned with your genetic profile.

What We Test

Targets the protein-coding regions of the genome — approximately 1–2% of total DNA where most known disease-related variants are located.

Focused analysis of specific genes relevant to cardiovascular health, drug metabolism (pharmacogenomics), hereditary cancer syndromes, and neurological conditions.

Identifies genetic variants affecting drug metabolism, helping to predict medication response and reduce the risk of adverse reactions.

Variants influencing folate metabolism, vitamin D processing, methylation capacity, and antioxidant enzyme function.

What Genetic Testing Is Not

Genetic testing provides information about predisposition and compatibility — it does not diagnose current disease, predict with certainty whether a condition will develop, or replace standard diagnostic workup. Results are interpreted in the context of your full clinical picture by our medical team.
